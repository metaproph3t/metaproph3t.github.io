About

Hello there! For some reason, you have stumbled upon my website. While you are here, I will regale you with my story.

My profile picture, generated by Stable Diffusion

I am an anonymous developer building on the Solana blockchain. Before anonymity, I worked for some prominent DeFi DAOs on Ethereum. Before that, I was a software engineer in web2.

While working for these organizations, I often felt malaise. It was uncomfortable to see decisions being made for reasons orthogonal to the organization's interests. For example, the web2 hiring managers tended to hire more engineers than they needed simply to bolster their own status (after all, "managed a team of 2,000" is much more impressive than "managed a team of 2," even if the first costs 1,000x more). And at the DAOs, I found that decisions were often made based on popularity contests rather than pros and cons lists.

Worse still, I felt myself being pulled in to this way of doing things. I was gradually learning that the best way to get ahead is to make your boss(es) happy, not necessarily what is in the group's interest. I was like one of those Soviet bureaucrats who figured out that if you're put in charge of chandelier production and your production is estimated by tonnage, the best course of action is to produce chandeliers so heavy "that they pull the ceilings down on our heads," in the words of Khruschev. And I desparately did not want to become a Soviet bureaucrat.

'You measured our output by tonnage. What did you expect?'

So, I decided to build an alternative organization. One where incentives were aligned such that someone maximizing for their own interest would produce a socially-beneficial outcome. This would be an organization that I would want to work for.

When I started exploring the design space, I thought I might be able to create such an organization with clever financial engineering. Maybe, for example, we could give decision-makers financial instruments that give them upside proportional to how well their decisions turns out. But for reasons of game theory, this was a dead end.

Even with balooning incentivization compensation, CEOs and professional managers' incentives are often insufficiently aligned with those of the shareholders. If anything, CEOs are paid too little, not too much!

So I turned to more untested designs. One that captivated my attention was futarchy. In a futarchy, markers make decisions, not appointed decision-makers. An ardent believer in markets, I started thinking about how futarchy could be implemented.

The result of this navel-gazing is the Meta-DAO. If all goes according to plan, the Meta-DAO will be an organization on Solana that uses futarchy, along with some other novel mechanisms, to make decisions. As you might expect from the name, the Meta-DAO will be comprised of many member DAOs, each of which manages a single product or service.

At the start, I hope to onboard Solana DeFi applications as members. Futarchy is well-suited to administrate DeFi applications because it is more resistant to the bribing and governance attacks that have plagued token-voting DAOs.

From there, who knows? Maybe the Meta-DAO will one day even reach the elusive network state status.
