The Meta-DAO Project

Why Ethereum's market position is more fragile than you think

The year is 2026. All applications and users have finally finished migrating from Ethereum Mainnet to a L2 rollup such as Optimism, Arbitrum, zkSync, or Scroll. Meanwhile, the price of ETH is plummeting. 'Wat mean?' asks @RyanSAdams on XXX (Elon got a bit carried away with naming). Well, let me tell you wat mean.

Life as pictured through ETH maxi goggles

"All I see is ETH, ETH everywhere"

As detailed in Vitalik's Incomplete Guide to Rollups, rollups consume L1 blockspace in three ways:

  1. storing state roots
  2. publishing transaction calldata
  3. (in ZK rollups) on-chain verification of the ZK-SNARK

Importantly, for any rollup with non-trivial scale, the vast majority of the L1 gas cost comes from publishing transaction calldata. ETH maxis like this because it means that Ethereum users can pay cheaper prices without reducing the total amount of ETH that is burnt. It's as if Ethereum were an airplane, and the L2 is a type of double-decker seat that allows double the number of people to sit in the plane: the plane can charge less per customer while still keeping their revenue the same or even increasing it.

Back to reality...

Reality goggles can be less fun.

But there's a problem in this thinking. What if the so-called rollups decide not to publish transaction calldata to Ethereum? After all, the business of transaction calldata storage doesn't have network effects. Within a single rollup, you could even have users that pay cheaper prices to publish to a data availability layer like Celestia interact with users who pay more expensive prices to publish a more expensive one like Ethereum Mainnet.

But, you say, with your lips trembling, "they wouldn't possibly think of doing that! That would ruin their Eth-alignment!" Well, they already have. Matter Labs has proposed zkPorter, their own data availability layer, which would obviate the need for publishing transaction calldata to Eth. And, in their own words, "Both parts will be composable and interoperable: contracts and accounts on the zkRollup side will be able to seamlessly interact with accounts on the zkPorter side, and vice versa. That’s right! From a user’s perspective, the only perceptible difference will be a 100x reduction in fees for zkPorter accounts."

Now, zkPorter is as of yet not a reality, but we've also been in an environment with relatively cheap gas fees. What happens in the next Zhupercycle? Well, all speculation, but here's my guess:

  • At least one of these rollups start giving the users the option to settle to a cheaper data availability layer
  • Users take that option, despite the pleadings of Eth maxis that this cheaper DA layer is marginally less secure
  • Ethereum Mainnet blockspace starts become more sparse, and burn compresses

To be clear, this will be good for the entire crypto industry. Ethereum is a bloated blockchain that has subsisted by its network effects. We deserve to live in an ecosystem that rewards good technology, and not just being early.
